Amazon, Walmart & eBay: A Comparative Guide for eCommerce Sellers

Preparing to Select a Platform
The online marketplace continues to expand, and it is essential to recognize that its vastness means not all platforms are suitable for every seller. With that in mind, Amazon, Walmart, and eBay are all excellent options for anyone looking to become an eCommerce seller. When selecting a platform, there is a potential impact on product exposure, profit margins, and brand development. This guide will evaluate these major platforms to help you determine the right one for your needs. We will review various aspects of these platforms, including features, fees, fulfillment, customer reach, and the seller experience.

Amazon: The Global Giant

Introduction

Amazon is the largest eCommerce platform in the world, boasting over 300 million customers across numerous countries. Amazon's advanced logistics and extensive loyal customer base make it an excellent choice for anyone looking to sell online.

Types of Seller Accounts and Plans

  • Individual: No monthly fee; pay-per-sale plan.
  • Professional: $39.99/month — ideal for scaling and advertising.
  • Enables bulk listings, reports, promotions, and ads.

Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA)

  • Faster delivery with Prime
  • Increased trust and conversions
  • Hands-free logistics management
  • Additional fees based on size, weight, and category

Advantages

  • Large global customer base
  • Streamlined fulfillment with FBA
  • Prime badge increases sales
  • Ad tools and A+ content availability

Disadvantages

  • Highly competitive
  • FBA costs can reduce profits
  • Strict performance metrics
  • Limited customer interaction

Walmart: America’s Retail Leader

Introduction

Walmart Marketplace is gaining rapid momentum, attracting over 150 million monthly visitors. It combines Walmart’s trust with third-party flexibility.

Eligibility and Onboarding

  • US-based business and tax ID
  • Unique products with valid UPCs
  • eCommerce experience is preferred

Walmart Fulfillment Services (WFS)

  • Two-day shipping
  • Buy Box advantage
  • Inventory and return management
  • Pricing based on size and weight

Benefits

  • No monthly fees
  • Less competition
  • Strong U.S. consumer trust
  • Opportunity for in-store exposure

Disadvantages

  • Selective and slow onboarding
  • Less responsive support
  • Limited international reach
  • Fewer branding tools

eBay: The Original eCommerce Platform

Introduction

eBay connects over 187 million buyers in 190 countries and supports auctions and fixed-price listings. Great for used, refurbished, and niche items.

Selling Flexibility

  • Individual listings
  • Store options (Basic, Premium, Anchor)

Seller Tools

  • Promoted Listings
  • Seller Hub Dashboard
  • Global Shipping Program
  • Easy returns and messaging

Advantages

  • Ideal for used and vintage products
  • Low startup costs
  • Less competition
  • Transparent fees

Disadvantages

  • Higher return rates
  • Not suited for large brands
  • Fewer automation tools
  • Bargain-focused audience

Compare Features: Amazon vs Walmart vs eBay

Buyer Reach and Growth

  • Amazon: 2.5 billion visits/month globally
  • Walmart: Strong U.S. omnichannel presence
  • eBay: Niche global reach

Ease of Entry

  • eBay: Easiest for beginners
  • Amazon: Complex setup with strict policies
  • Walmart: Business proof required

Branding Tools Comparison

  • Amazon: Limited and strict
  • Walmart: Moderate and strict
  • eBay: Flexible for SMBs

Deep Dive into Each Platform

Amazon’s Best-Selling Categories

  • Electronics and accessories
  • Books and media
  • Health and personal care
  • Home and kitchen
  • Private label products

Walmart’s Top Categories

  • Household consumables
  • Clothing
  • Outdoor and sports
  • Office supplies
  • Baby products

eBay's Specialty Categories

  • Collectibles and memorabilia
  • Refurbished electronics
  • Vintage clothing
  • Discontinued items

Platform-Specific Selling Strategies

Amazon

  • Choose less competitive niches
  • Get early reviews
  • Use A+ content
  • Use Subscribe & Save
  • Enroll in Brand Registry

Walmart

  • Offer fast shipping
  • Undercut Amazon prices
  • Avoid stockouts
  • Use WFS for smoother logistics
  • Update listings weekly

eBay

  • Detailed product descriptions
  • Accept Best Offers
  • Offer returns
  • Use auctions for rare items
  • Build positive reviews

eCommerce Trends 2025 and Beyond

  • AI & automation for pricing, inventory, and ads
  • Sustainability drives consumer loyalty
  • Voice and mobile shopping dominate
  • Social commerce via influencers and video

Fee Structure Overview

Amazon

  • $39.99/month (Professional)
  • Referral: 6%–45% (avg. 15%)
  • FBA and storage fees

Walmart

  • No monthly fee
  • Referral: 6%–20%

eBay

  • 10%–15% final value fee
  • $0.35 insertion fee after 250 listings

Platform Selection Guide

  • Choose Amazon: For international scaling and FBA use
  • Choose Walmart: U.S.-focused with efficient logistics
  • Choose eBay: For used items and low-risk entry

Universal Success Strategies

1. Optimize Listings

  • Use keywords in titles and descriptions
  • High-quality images
  • Bullet points for clarity

2. Monitor Inventory and Prices

  • Avoid going out of stock
  • Use competitor pricing data
  • Implement repricing tools

3. Prioritize Reviews and Support

  • Request feedback
  • Handle disputes professionally
  • Fast and reliable shipping

4. Use Advertising

  • Amazon Ads
  • Walmart Sponsored Products
  • eBay Promoted Listings

Conclusion

Long-term success on any platform depends on consistency, data, and customer-centric practices. eCommerce is essential, not optional. Success comes from agility, strategy, and continuous improvement.

Final Thoughts

  • Amazon: For automation and scale
  • Walmart: For U.S.-focused sellers
  • eBay: For niche or used products
